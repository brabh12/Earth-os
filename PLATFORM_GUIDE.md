# 🌍 Earth OS: Platform Usage Guide

Welcome to the **Earth OS** User Manual. This document explains the core functionalities of the platform, the intended audience, and technical definitions to help you navigate our planetary intelligence system.

---

## 🎯 Who is Earth OS for?

Earth OS is designed for a diverse ecosystem of environmental defenders:
*   **🌍 Environmental NGOs**: To identify and prioritize high-risk areas for intervention.
*   **🔬 Conservation Scientists**: To monitor real-time satellite telemetry and analyze regional health trends.
*   **🤝 Global Volunteers**: To coordinate field-based missions and provide "Proof of Impact" verification.
*   **🌲 Policy Makers**: To visualize data-driven arguments for environmental protection.

## 🔐 Getting Started: Secure Uplink

Before accessing the planetary data, every user must establish a **Secure Uplink** (Authentication):
1.  **Sign In / Sign Up**: Use the encrypted portal to create a profile.
2.  **Role Assignment**: Your mission capabilities (Volunteer vs. Admin) are determined by your credentials stored in our Supabase backend.
3.  **Connection Status**: Once connected, the top header will display "SYSTEM STATUS: OPTIMAL" and your specific "USER_ROLE".

## 🚀 How to Use the Platform

The software operates as a high-performance **Command & Control** center. All features are accessible through the **Planetary Navigation Sidebar** on the left.

### 1. Initial Assessment
Check the **Overview** dashboard to see the current **Global Health Index**. If it drops below 75%, immediate intervention is required.

### 2. Threat Detection
Navigate to the **Planetary Map**. Use the **Copernicus Sentinel-2** layer to see real-world satellite imagery. Look for "Anomaly Pulse" markers (red/yellow circles) indicating deforestation, water scarcity, or pollution.

### 3. AI Deep Scan
Go to the **Active Issues** page. Select a specific anomaly and click **"INVOKE AI ANALYSIS"**. The **Earth Intelligence Core** will analyze the multispectral data and generate a custom 3-stage strategy.

### 4. Mission Deployment
Confirm the AI's strategy to move it into the **Missions** registry. Here, you can coordinate resources and volunteers. 

### 5. Verification & Tracking
Submit field reports (images/data) to the **Verification Portal**. Once the AI confirms the mission's success, view your global contribution in the **Impact Tracking** page.

## 📊 Feature Glossary

### 📡 Copernicus Sentinel-2 Layer
A live satellite feed from the European Space Agency. It provides high-resolution imagery every 5 days, allowing us to see real changes on the ground almost in real-time.

### 🧠 AI Command Console
Powered by advanced Large Language Models, this unit translates complex satellite telemetry into human-readable intervention plans. It calculates confidence scores and projects environmental gains.

### 🛑 Autonomous Anomaly Registry
An internal engine that scans the globe for specific signatures (UV shifts, heat spikes, canopy gaps) and automatically flags them for human review.

## 📐 Technical Units & Metrics Explained

### **TFLOPS (Teraflops)**
*   **Meaning**: One trillion floating-point operations per second.
*   **In Earth OS**: This represents the computing power currently dedicated to planetary scanning. Higher TFLOPS means the AI is processing more satellite imagery and sensory data per second.

### **KM² (Square Kilometers)**
*   **In Earth OS**: Used to measure the extent of environmental damage (e.g., lost forest) or the size of a recovery zone.

### **MT (Metric Tons)**
*   **In Earth OS**: Specifically refers to **CO2 Sequestration Gain**. It measures how much carbon dioxide is being successfully removed from the atmosphere by the forest we protect or restore.

### **Confidence Score (%)**
*   How certain the AI is that an anomaly is real and not a sensor artifact. 90%+ is considered "Actionable Intelligence."

### **OPERATIVES (Personnel)**
*   **In Earth OS**: Refers to the recommended number of individuals (volunteers, scientists, or workers) required for a mission. This number is estimated by AI to ensure the intervention team is sufficiently sized for the specific geographic challenge.

### **Global Stability Index (%)**
*   **In Earth OS**: A weighted average of planetary health, taking into account current air pollution, CO2 levels, deforestation rates, and active anomalies. A score below 70% triggers an automated "Planetary Alert."

## 🖥️ Page Explanations

### **1. Home / Overview**
Your "Mission Control." It shows global health summaries, the latest system logs, and a distribution of current threats (e.g., % of deforestation vs. air pollution).

### **2. Planetary Map**
A digital twin of Earth. Toggle layers (Satellite, Dark Matter, Heatmaps) and zoom into specific coordinates to see the actual state of the environment.

### **3. Active Issues**
The central registry of detected threats. Each entry contains specific coordinates, severity levels, and a direct link to the AI scanning unit.

### **4. Missions**
The tactical operations portal. It lists active field interventions, resource allocation requirements, and the status of volunteers deployed to the site.

### **5. Impact Tracking**
The "Victory Board." It visualizes historical recovery trends over the last 12 months, showing the total area recovered and CO2 gains achieved by the Earth OS community.
